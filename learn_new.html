Module 1: Fundamental Physics

The Silicon Atom to CMOS

Every chip begins with the manipulation of silicon. From P-type and N-type doping, we create the CMOS inverter—the basic building block of all digital logic.

Dynamic Switching Theory

CMOS logic consumes zero static power. Energy is only dissipated during the switching (0 → 1 or 1 → 0) phase.

💡 Concept: Threshold Voltage (Vth) - The gate-source voltage required to create a conduction path between source and drain.
Module 2: Netlist-to-GDSII

The Physical Design Flow

This is the core process of chipmaking. Transforming the synthesized netlist into a manufacturable physical layout follows a strict, multi-stage pipeline.

1. Floorplanning

The chip's blueprint. Deciding die area, I/O placement, and macro positioning to minimize congestion and IR drop.

2. Placement

Positioning millions of standard cells within row boundaries. Aim: Minimized wirelength and timing satisfaction.

3. Clock Tree (CTS)

Building the H-Tree or Mesh to deliver clock signals to all registers with minimum global skew and latency.

4. Routing

Final metal interconnect design. Global and Detail routing phases that fulfill all logical net requirements.

Module 3: The Goal

Tape-out & Verification

The final stage. Before manufacturing, the design must undergo rigorous sign-off checks to ensure it will function correctly and have high yield.

PV: Physical Verification

DRC (Rule checks), LVS (Layout matched to Netlist), and ERC (Electrical checks).

STA: Static Timing

Verifying setup and hold times across all corners to ensure operating frequency targets.
