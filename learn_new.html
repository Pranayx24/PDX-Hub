Module 1 Theory + Fundamentals

CMOS & ASIC Design

The bedrock of modern electronics. Understanding the transition from the Silicon atom to complex CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) logic gates and the distinction between Full-Custom and Semi-Custom (ASIC) design methodologies.

ASIC Design vs FPGA

ASICs (Application Specific Integrated Circuits) offer superior performance, lower power, and smaller area compared to FPGAs, but involve higher NRE (Non-Recurring Engineering) costs and longer time-to-market.

  • • High Performance (GHz+)
  • • High Volume Profitability
  • • Custom Layout Density
  • • Optimized Power Profile
Module 2

Logic Synthesis (1-3)

Synthesis is the process of converting High-Level (RTL) code—Verilog or VHDL—into a Gate-Level Netlist while optimizing for Power, Performance, and Area (PPA).

STAGE 1

Translation

Converting HDL into GTECH (Generic Technology) format. The design is mapped to functional architectural blocks.

STAGE 2

Logic Optimization

Applying Boolean reductions and restructuring to minimize gate count and path delays based on user constraints.

STAGE 3

Technology Mapping

Selecting the final cells from the Foundry Library (.lib) to replace GTECH gates, ensuring the netlist adheres to physical DRC rules.

Module 4

Sanity Checks

Before starting the P&R flow, "Garbage In, Garbage Out" must be avoided. The following checks are mandatory:

  • ✅ Check Netlist: Verifying floating pins, multiple drivers, and unresolved references.
  • ✅ Check Timing: Ensuring SDC constraints correctly cover all Reg-to-Reg and I/O paths.
  • ✅ Check Library: Matching logical (.lib) and physical (LEF) pins exactly.
Module 5

PD Flow & Terminology

"Know the jargon or get rejected." Below is the critical vocabulary for a Tier-1 PD Engineer.

WNS / TNS

Worst Negative Slack (The single worst violation) vs Total Negative Slack (Sum of all violations).

Skew / Latency

Clock skew (diff in arrival times) vs Clock latency (delay from source to sink).

Utilization

Ratio of standard cell area to core area. Ideally 60-70% for routability.

Module 6

Floorplan Strategy

RAM_1
ROM
CORE AREA

Floorplanning defines the chip's physical limits and internal partition boundaries. Key decisions include Aspect Ratio (w/h), Core/Die sizing, and Macro placement while avoiding congestion near I/O pins.

Module 7

Power Planning

The Power Grid (Vanguard of Integrity)

Ensuring uniform power distribution across the die using Rings, Stripes (on higher metal layers), and Rails (on M1). Goal: Minimize Static and Dynamic IR drop through robust PDN (Power Distribution Network) design.

Module 8

STA Fundamentals

// SETUP Formula
T_data < T_clk + T_period - T_setup
// HOLD Formula
T_data > T_clk + T_hold

STA (Static Timing Analysis) checks every path for timing compliance without requiring test vectors. It is the most critical sign-off metric for frequency targets.

Module 9

Placement Engine

Global Placement

Distributes cells uniformly across rows, ignoring overlaps to minimize total net wirelength.

Detailed Placement

Snaps cells to the site grid, resolves all overlaps, and prepares the design for CTS.

Module 10

Clock Tree Synthesis

Q: Why not route clocks during the routing phase?
A: Clocks require minimum skew and latency; routing handles signals with less stringent requirements.

H-Tree vs Fishbone

Building the distribution network to millions of flip-flops while managing power consumption (the clock consumes ~40% of chip power).

Module 11

Routing Phase

Global Routing

Allocates net paths across G-Cells (Global segments) to plan density.

Detail Routing

Actual metal shape creation on manufacture tracks, ensuring zero DRC violations.

Module 12

Optimization Tech

Vt Swapping: Changing cells to Low-Vt for speed or High-Vt for power.
Gate Sizing: Modifying drive strength to fix drive capability issues.
Module 13

Process Variations

Understanding PVT (Process, Voltage, Temperature) corners and the impact of OCV (On-Chip Variation) on sub-7nm designs.
