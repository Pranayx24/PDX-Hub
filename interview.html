Interview Master Prep

Q1: What is the difference between Setup Time and Hold Time? +
Setup Time is the minimum time before the clock edge that the data must be stable. If violated, it causes timing failure.
Hold Time is the minimum time after the clock edge data must remain stable. Hold violations cannot be fixed by reducing clock frequency.
Q2: What are 'Antenna Rules' in Physical Design? +
During manufacturing, metal wires act as antennas. They collect electric charge during plasma etching, which can discharge through the gate oxide of transistors, permanently damaging them. Fixes include adding antenna diodes or metal hopping (moving to higher layers).
Q3: Why is Clock Tree Synthesis (CTS) important? +
CTS ensures that the clock signal reaches all sequential elements at roughly the same time. The main goals are to minimize global skew, minimize insertion delay, and keep power consumption low through efficient buffer placement.
Q4: What is Crosstalk and how it affects timing? +
Crosstalk is the unwanted interaction between adjacent wires due to coupling capacitance. It can cause Delta Delay (speeding up or slowing down signals) or Glitches (changing logic state).
Q5: Explain OCV (On-Chip Variation). +
OCV accounts for variations in process, voltage, and temperature across different regions of the same die. It's modeled using derating factors to make timing analysis more pessimistic and ensure robust manufacturing.
