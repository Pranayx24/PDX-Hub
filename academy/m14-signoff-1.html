Phase III: Advanced

Sign-off Physical

The final gate. Physical verification (PV) ensures your layout is manufacturable and logically correct.

1. DRC and LVS Verification

Physical verification checks use industry-standard tools (like Mentor Calibre) to compare the final GDSII layout against the original netlist and silicon manufacturing rules.

The Verification Twins:

  • DRC (Design Rule Check): Ensuring metal spacing, width, and via rules are honored. At 7nm, this includes Multi-Patterning (LELE) rules.
  • LVS (Layout vs Schematic): Confirming that the physical transistors and connections in the layout match the logical netlist.
  • Density & Dummy Fill: Ensuring every region of the die has a minimum concentration of metal (e.g., 20-30%) to prevent "CMP (Chemical Mechanical Polishing)" variations. The tool inserts "Dummies" (floating small square metal shapes) to reach this limit.

2. Reliability: ESD and Latch-up

  • ESDProtection: Every I/O pad must have high-current diodes to drain electrostatic discharge (static electricity) before it blows the internal transistors.
  • Latch-up Prevention: "Tap Cells" or substrate/well taps must be placed every ~30μm in the regular cell rows to prevent the formation of parasitic thyristors and silicon meltdown.
  • OASIS Format: Modern alternative to GDSII that uses advanced compression to handle the massive terabyte-scale layout files for sub-7nm chips.

Interview focus: LVS Failure

"What kind of errors can cause an LVS mismatch?"

Answer: Typical LVS errors include missing connectivity, mismatched pin names, and shorted nets. Often, these arise from "soft connections" where pins aren't technically touching in the layout despite being logically connected in the tool.
