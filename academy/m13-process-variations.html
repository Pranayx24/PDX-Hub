Phase III: Advanced

Process Variations

The reality of silicon. Modeling variations in semiconductor manufacturing to ensure 100% yield.

1. PVT Corners: Process, Voltage, Temperature

Silicon never matches the ideal model exactly. We analyze the design at extreme "Corners" (Worst Case, Best Case) to ensure it works across all manufacturing tolerances.

The PVT Hierarchy:

  • SS Core (Slow-Slow): Minimum voltage, maximum temperature, and slowest transistors. Primary for Setup verification.
  • FF Core (Fast-Fast): Maximum voltage, minimum temperature, and fastest transistors. Primary for Hold verification.
  • Temperature Inversion: A phenomenon at sub-28nm where delay actually increases as temperature decreases.

2. OCV (On-Chip Variation)

OCV accounts for localized variations within the same die. Modern signs use POCV (Parametric OCV) that models timing as a statistical distribution (Sigma-based) rather than fixed derates.

Interview focus: Temperature Inversion

"Why is timing analysis at low temperature critical for sub-28nm designs?"

Answer: Due to temperature inversion, many timing violations (both setup and hold) can occur at extremely low temperatures (0°C to -40°C) which were previously ignored in older nodes.
