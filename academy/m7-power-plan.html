Phase II: Execution

Power Planning

The delivery of energy. Building the power grid (PDN) to sustain billions of switching transistors.

1. The Power Distribution Network (PDN)

Every chip needs VDD (Power) and VSS (Ground). Delivery of these rails must be uniform to prevent IR drop (voltage decay) and Electromigration (metal failure).

The Three Hierarchy Levels:

  • Rings: Large peripheral metal bands around the core or macros that serve as the main power source.
  • Stripes: Vertical or horizontal metal bands across the core, typically on higher (thicker) metal layers (e.g., M7, M8).
  • Rails: Standard cell power and ground rails on the first layer (M1) that provide local power to every cell.

2. Dynamic and Static IR Drop

IR Drop is the reduction in voltage between the power source and the transistor. Too much drop means slower switching and potential logic failure.

  • Static IR Drop: Caused by the DC resistance of the metal grid. Fixed by adding thicker/more stripes.
  • Dynamic IR Drop: Caused by simultaneous switching of millions of transistors (peak current). Mitigated by adding Decoupling Capacitors (Decaps).

Interview focus: Decoupling Cells

"What is a Decap cell, and why is it essential for Power Integrity?"

Answer: Decap cells are capacitors placed throughout the core that store local charge. During peak switching events, they discharge to provide current locally, preventing a sudden dip in the VDD rail (Dynamic IR drop).
