Phase II: Execution

Power Planning

The delivery of energy. Building the power grid (PDN) to sustain billions of switching transistors.

1. The Power Delivery Network (PDN)

Power Planning translates the UPF power intent into a physical mesh of metal. Unlike signal nets, power nets must carry massive current (Amperes) across the whole chip without losing voltage.

PDN Hierarchy:

  • Power Ring: A continuous ring around the chip or macros to distribute VDD/VSS.
  • Power Straps (Mesh): Vertical and horizontal stripes across the core. Wider straps solve voltage drop but create more routing blockage for signal nets.
  • Standard Cell Rails: Continuous metal (usually M1) inside the rows where standard cell VDD/VSS pins connect directly.
Static vs Dynamic IR Drop

The voltage arriving at a gate's pin is always less than the supply (VDD). Static IR drop is caused by the average current flow through the resistance of the metal. Dynamic IR drop is caused by millions of gates switching at the exact same time (like the clock edge), resulting in massive transient current spikes that can cause gates to fail to switch correctly.

2. Electromigration (EM) Criticality

Electromigration occurs when current density is so high that metal atoms are physically pushed away, creating "voids" (opens) or "hillocks" (shorts).

  • Fix: Increase the width of the strap or the number of vias connecting metal layers.
  • Current Density (J): J = I / Area. Power planning ensures J < J_foundry_limit across all metal segments.
  • RedHawk Analysis: Using industry-standard power sign-off tools to identify weak spots in the mesh before tape-out.

Interview focus: Decoupling Cells

"What is a Decap cell, and why is it essential for Power Integrity?"

Answer: Decap cells are capacitors placed throughout the core that store local charge. During peak switching events, they discharge to provide current locally, preventing a sudden dip in the VDD rail (Dynamic IR drop).
