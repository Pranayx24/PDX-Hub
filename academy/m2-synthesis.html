Logic Synthesis (RTL → Netlist)

The critical bridge between code and hardware. Learn how RTL becomes a gate-level structure optimized for PPA.

1. The Synthesis Core Process

Synthesis is the translation of behavioral Hardware Description Language (HDL), such as Verilog or VHDL, into a structural gate-level netlist. This gate-level netlist consists of standard cells from the target technology library.

RTL (Verilog)
→
GTECH
→
Netlist (.v)
Key Insight: Logical Synthesis does not account for physical wires. It uses "Wire Load Models" (WLM) to estimate delays, which are often optimistic for sub-7nm designs.

Action: For modern designs, Physical-Aware Synthesis (e.g., Genus-iSpatial or DC-NXT) is used.

2. Translation, Optimization, & Mapping

The synthesis process is divided into three distinct phases:

  • Translation: Converting HDL to Generic technology cells (GTECH).
  • Logic Optimization: Boolean logic simplification, eliminating redundant gates, and path restructuring.
  • Technology Mapping: Replacing generic gates with actual standard cells from the foundry's .lib file that meet timing and area constraints.

3. Synthesizing for PPA (SDC)

Constraints govern the synthesis output. The Synopsys Design Constraints (SDC) file defines:

Clock Definition

Defining the operating frequency (create_clock). Everything depends on this period.

Input/Output Delays

Accounting for delays outside the chip at the PCB level.

Interview focus: GTECH and Netlist

"Why is a netlist preferred over RTL for Physical Design tools?"

Answer: Physical Design tools require structural maps of actual foundry-provided cells (LEF/LIB). RTL is too abstract; a netlist provides specific gate drive strengths and Vt values needed for timing and power analysis.
