Logic Synthesis (RTL → Netlist)

The critical bridge between code and hardware. Learn how RTL becomes a gate-level structure optimized for PPA.

1. The Synthesis Core Process

Synthesis is the translation of behavioral Hardware Description Language (HDL) into a structural gate-level netlist. Modern synthesis is "Physical-Aware", meaning it uses a preliminary floorplan and RC models to ensure the resulting netlist is routable.

Advanced Concept: Logical Effort

The method of Logical Effort helps designers estimate the delay of a logic gate by splitting it into three factors: d = g·h + p.

  • g (Logical Effort): Intrinsic complexity of the gate (e.g., NAND2 > Inverter).
  • h (Electrical Effort): External load capacitance / Input capacitance (Fan-out).
  • p (Parasitic Delay): Internal capacitance of the gate itself.

2. Advanced Optimization Techniques

The synthesis tool doesn't just map gates; it performs surgery on the logic to meet SDC constraints:

  • Clock Gating Insertion: Automatically adding ICG (Integrated Clock Gate) cells to disable the clock tree for registers when their enable signal is low. This is the #1 method for dynamic power reduction.
  • Retiming: Moving flip-flops across combinatorial boundaries (pushing/pulling through gates) to balance slack on either side of the register.
  • Deglitching: Restructuring logic trees to reduce switching activity caused by race conditions in combinatorial paths.

3. MCMM (Multi-Corner Multi-Mode) Synthesis

In modern nodes, synthesis must satisfy timing across multiple "scenarios" simultaneously.

Modes

Functional Mode, Scan Shift Mode, Capture Mode, and Sleep Mode. Each has its own SDC constraints.

Corners

PVT (Process, Voltage, Temperature) combinations like SSG (Slow-Slow Global) or FFG (Fast-Fast Global).

The synthesis engine must ensure that the Worst Negative Slack (WNS) is minimized across all scenarios without creating a massive area penalty.

Interview focus: Synthesis Optimization

"What is the difference between Flattening and Structuring in logic optimization?"

Answer: Flattening converts combinatorial logic into a Sum-of-Products (SOP) form to minimize delay (critical path). Structuring identifies shared logic sub-expressions to reduce area (total gate count).

Interview focus: Retiming

"When would you NOT want to use retiming during synthesis?"

Answer: Retiming changes the number of registers between two points and their names. It should be avoided on boundary registers (I/Os) or logic that must match a golden reference for formal verification (LEC) unless the LEC tool supports retiming checks.
