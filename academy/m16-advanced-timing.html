Phase III: Advanced

Advanced Timing

Crosstalk & Noise. Deep dive into signal integrity and the impact of wire-to-wire capacitance on silicon timing.

1. Crosstalk Delay (SI)

Signal Integrity (SI) issues are caused by the coupling capacitance between adjacent wires. When an "Aggressor" net switches, it can inject charge into a neighboring "Victim" net.

Crosstalk Outcomes:

  • Crosstalk Delay: Switching in the opposite direction (Aggressor ↑, Victim ↓) increases delay. Switching in the same direction (Aggressor ↑, Victim ↑) decreases delay.
  • Crosstalk Glitch: A switching aggressor induces a voltage spike on a static victim net. If large enough, this can flip a gate's state erroneously.

2. Signal Integrity Mitigation

We solve crosstalk by increasing the space between critical nets or adding ground shielding. For high-switching nets (like clocks), shielding with VSS wires on both sides is mandatory.

Interview focus: Clock Shielding

"Why is clock shielding required for sub-7nm designs?"

Answer: Clocks switch 100% of the time, making them the most aggressive source of noise. In tight-geometry nodes (sub-7nm), the lateral capacitance is so high that an unshielded clock can easily cause timing failures in neighboring data nets.
