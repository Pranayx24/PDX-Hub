CMOS & ASIC Design

The masterclass for Physical Design engineers. From semiconductor physics to high-performance ASIC methodology.

1. The CMOS Transistor Basics

CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) is the technology used to construct integrated circuits. It uses pairs of P-type and N-type MOSFETs to perform logic functions. As we move to nodes below 22nm, traditional planar MOSFETs have given way to 3D structures like FinFETs and GAAFETs (Gate-All-Around).

Advanced Device Physics: FinFET vs Planar

In planar MOSFETs, short-channel effects (SCE) lead to significant leakage as the gate loses control over the channel. FinFETs wrap the gate around three sides of a vertical "fin" channel, providing superior electrostatic control and enabling lower VDD operation.

Power Analysis Formulas:
  • P_dynamic = α · C · VDD² · f (Switching Power)
  • P_short_circuit = I_peak · VDD · (t_rise + t_fall) / 2
  • P_leakage = I_leak · VDD (Sub-threshold + Gate Leakage)

α = Switching activity factor, C = Load capacitance, f = Frequency.

2. Silicon Fabrication Flow (FEOL to BEOL)

ASIC manufacturing is split into two major phases:

  • FEOL (Front-End-of-Line): Formation of individual transistors directly on the silicon. Includes well formation, gate stacking, and source/drain engineering.
  • BEOL (Back-End-of-Line): Creation of interconnects (Metal layers and Vias) that connect the transistors. This is where Physical Design plays its largest role.
  • WLP (Wafer Level Packaging): Modern techniques like TSV (Through Silicon Vias) for 3D-IC stacking.

3. ASIC Methodology & Lifecycle

Developing an ASIC follows a "V-Model" or iterative loop from Spec to Tape-out.

Pre-Silicon Phase

RTL Design, Functional Verification, Synthesis, and Physical Design (P&R). This is where the virtual model is perfected.

Post-Silicon Phase

Wafer testing, Packaging, and Bench validation. Ensuring the manufactured chip meets the power and speed targets simulated in the Cadence/Synopsys tools.

Interview focus: Advanced CMOS

"Why can't we keep scaling VDD linearly with transistor size?"

Answer: Sub-threshold leakage increases exponentially as threshold voltage (Vth) is lowered to maintain performance. VDD must stay high enough above Vth to provide sufficient drive current, creating a "Power Wall".

Interview focus: Body Effect

"How does the Body Effect impact a NAND gate vs an Inverter?"

Answer: In 2-input NAND gates, the NMOS transistors are stacked. The source of the top NMOS is not connected to ground, creating a non-zero Vsb (Source-to-Body voltage). This increases the Vth of the top NMOS, making the gate slower than a simple inverter.
