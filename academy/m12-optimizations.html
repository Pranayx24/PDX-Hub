Phase III: Advanced

Optimization Tech

The refinement of silicon. Using advanced techniques like Vt-swapping and gate-sizing to meet PPA targets.

1. Post-Route Optimization

Once routing is complete, the tool has precise RC (Resistance-Capacitance) values for every wire. Optimization now focuses on surgical fixes to timing and power violations.

Power-Performance-Area (PPA) Levers:

  • Vt-Swapping: Changing a cell from High-Vt (Low Leakage, Slow) to Low-Vt (High Leakage, Fast) specifically on critical timing paths.
  • Gate Sizing: Increasing the drive strength of a gate to drive higher capacitance downstream net at the cost of area and power.
  • Buffer Insertion: Breaking long, high-RC wires by inserting buffers to restore signal integrity.

2. Leakage Power Recovery

After timing is met, the tool performs "Leakage Recovery" by swapping Low-Vt cells back to High-Vt on paths with positive slack. This can reduce total power consumption by 20-30%.

Interview focus: Vt Selection

"What is the disadvantage of using only Low-Vt (LVT) cells in your design?"

Answer: LVT cells have a very thin gate oxide, leading to high sub-threshold leakage current. A design using only LVT will exceed power specifications even when data is not switching.
