Phase III: Advanced

Sign-off Reliability

The endurance of silicon. Analyzing EM and IR drop to ensure your chip doesn't burn out over time.

1. Electromigration (EM) Analysis

EM is the literal transport of material caused by current flow. If a wire carries too much current, it will eventually "melt" or break, leading to silicon failure within years or even months.

EM Mitigation:

Fixing EM violations requires increasing the wire width or adding more redundant vias to share the current load. Higher metal layers (thicker) are always used for high-current power nets.

2. Dynamic IR Drop Sign-off

Using specialized tools (e.g., Ansys RedHawk or Cadence Voltus) to model peak power switching. If IR drop exceeds ~10% of VDD, logic transitions become too slow, and timing paths will fail despite what STA says.

Interview focus: EM vs IR Drop

"What is the difference between a Signal EM violation and a Power EM violation?"

Answer: Power EM is caused by steady DC current flowing through VDD/VSS rails. Signal EM is caused by high-frequency AC switching current in local interconnects. Both require wider wires or more vias but are analyzed by different tools.
