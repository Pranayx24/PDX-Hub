Phase II: Execution

Placement Engine

The positioning of logic. Distributing millions of standard cells within the core to achieve optimal PPA.

1. Global vs Detailed Placement

Placement occurs in two primary stages. The EDA tool uses force-directed algorithms to pull cells toward their logical neighbors while pushing them apart to avoid density hotspots.

Placement Stages:

  • Global Placement (GP): Cells are distributed uniformly, but they may overlap. The goal is to minimize total net wirelength.
  • Legalization: Snapping cells into "rows" and "sites" defined by the technology LEF. No overlaps allowed.
  • Detailed Placement (DP): Fine-tuning cell positions locally to reduce wirelength or congestion after legalization.

2. Congestion Mitigation

Congestion happens when more wires need to pass through a region than there are available metal tracks. Mitigations include:

  • Padding: Adding virtual area around cells to force them further apart.
  • Partial Blockage: Restricting cell placement in dense regions.
  • Magnet Placement: Pulling cells closer to I/O ports or specific macros to shorten critical wires.

Interview focus: Congestion

"What is the difference between Pin Density and Cell Density?"

Answer: Cell Density refers to how much core area is occupied by cells. Pin Density refers to the concentration of connection points (pins). A region with low cell density but many high-pin-count cells (complex gates) can still have huge routing congestion.
