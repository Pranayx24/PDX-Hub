1. The Macro-Flow: Netlist to GDSII

Physical Design (PD) is an iterative process. Each stage builds on the previous one, and design decisions often flow backwards (e.g., routing congestion might force a floorplan change).

Primary PD Objectives:

  • Floorplanning: Determine the best core shape and pin locations.
  • Placement: Map cells with minimal wirelength and zero overlap.
  • CTS (Clock Tree Synthesis): Deliver the clock to every pin with zero skew and minimum insertion delay.
  • Routing: Perform physical metal connections without DRC violations.

2. Key Academic Jargon for a PD Engineer

  • WNS (Worst Negative Slack): Refers to the most severe timing violation in the design. If clock frequency is 1GHz (1ns) and the longest path is 1.2ns, your WNS is -200ps.
  • TNS (Total Negative Slack): The sum of all slacks that are negative. This is the indicator of how 'messy' your design's timing is. A high TNS with a low WNS suggests a few small fixes are needed on many paths.
  • Utilization (Core Efficiency): Standard cell area / core area. High utilization (75%+) means the chip is smaller and cheaper, but it creates "Routing Congestion" where there aren't enough metal tracks to connect the cells.

Interview focus: The "Why" of the Flow

"Why do we perform Clock Tree Synthesis (CTS) after Placement and not before?"

Answer: CTS requires precise locations of every flip-flop to balance skew efficiently. Until Placement is done, we don't know where the sinks are, so balanced skew is impossible to calculate reliably.
